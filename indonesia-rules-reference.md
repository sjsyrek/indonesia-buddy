Compiled from four sources:

- **[S1]** Gist summary — https://gist.github.com/instinctive/ad00373716cc4ccd0a695a2afe064c53
- **[S2]** Cardboard Appendix — https://cardboardappendix.com/games/indonesia/rules-summary/

Where sources conflict, the official rulebook [S4] is treated as the primary authority.

## 1. Shipping Payment Direction

**Who pays whom:** The **production company owner receives revenue from the bank**, then **pays the shipping company owners** out of that revenue.

- [S4] "The owner of the production company gets a fixed fee depending on the type of goods sold: 20 for rice, 25 for spice, and so on. Out of this money, he has to pay 5 for each ship he uses to the owner of the respective shipping companies."
- [S1] "The bank pays the operating player for the good." Then: "Add up all the cargo cubes used and multiply by 5. The operating player pays that amount to the bank." Then: "Distribute the cargo cubes to the players who own the ships. Each cube is worth 5."
- [S2] "They must pay the owner(s) of the shipping companies they used $5 per good that passed through each ship."

**Conclusion:** The bank pays the production company the good's value. The production company owner then pays 5 Rp per ship used to the shipping company owners. The production company owner bears the shipping cost.

## 2. R&D Tracks

**There are 5 separate R&D tracks per player, plus 1 company-level hull track.**

Per the official rules [S4], each player places a marker on each R&D track. The tracks are:

| # | Track | What It Controls |
|---|-------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Slots** | Maximum number of companies a player may own at any time. Merged multi-deed companies occupy only one slot. [S4][S1][S2] |
| 2 | **Mergers** | Maximum number of company deeds that may be involved in a merger announced by that player. At level 1, cannot announce mergers. [S4][S1] |
| 3 | **Hull** (per player) | Number of goods each ship belonging to that player's companies may transport per production company during operations. [S4] "Hull Player indicates the number of goods each ship belonging to a company of that player may transport for each production company during the operations phase." |
| 4 | **Expansion** | Maximum number of areas/ships a production or shipping company may expand during operations. [S4][S1] |
| 5 | **Turn Order Bid** | Multiplier applied to the player's cash bid during the Turn Order phase. [S4][S1][S2] |

**Hull is a player-level track, not a separate "level 3 unlock."** Each player's ships share that player's hull capacity. One ship of each company is placed on a Company Hull track to show at-a-glance capacity. [S4]

- [S2] lists 6 tracks including "Hull Player" as a separate track — this is the same as the Hull track in [S4].
- [S1] describes it as "Hull Capacity R&D."

**Conclusion:** 5 personal R&D tracks. Hull is one of them. There is no unified single R&D track.

## 3. Turn Order Determination

**Turn order is determined by bidding cash each year.**

- [S4] "Each player, starting with the first one in order of play, bids an amount of cash once. A bid of 0 is allowed. This cash is placed in the 'Bank' box on his player aid."
- [S4] "Whoever has the highest bid gets to be first in the order of play... In case of a tie between two players, their order with respect to each other remains unchanged."
- [S4] "If players have invested in the 'Turn Order Bid' on the R&D track, their bid is multiplied by the corresponding amount."
- [S1] Confirms: bids multiplied by Turn Order Bid R&D; ties maintain relative order.

**Conclusion:** Players bid cash (multiplied by their Turn Order Bid R&D level). Highest effective bid goes first. Ties preserve prior relative order. Bid money goes to the "Bank" box permanently.

## 4. City Maximum Size

**Maximum city size is 3.**

- [S4] "A size 2 city needs 2 of every good to become a size 3 city."
- [S1] "12 size 1 cities, 8 size 2 cities, and 3 size 3 cities."
- [S4] Component list: "12 city size 1, 8 city size 2, 3 city size 3 markers."

**Conclusion:** Cities go from size 1 to size 2 to size 3 (maximum). The limit is the physical supply of tokens: 12/8/3.

---

## 5. City Growth Condition

**A city must receive its size in goods of EVERY type currently produced on the board.**

- [S4] "a size 1 city needs one of every good on the board to become a size 2 city. A size 2 city needs 2 of every good to become a size 3 city."
- [S4] "Note that a city needs only those goods that are being produced on the board. In the first years, there is never any rubber, siap faji or oil, and it can even happen that there are no spices."
- [S1] "Each size 1 and size 2 city that received its size in goods for every type that is produced on the board will grow."
- [S2] "Cities require 1x its level of each good that can be produced to grow."

**Conclusion:** A size-N city grows if it received N goods of EVERY type currently produced anywhere on the board. Not N different goods — N of EACH type. Goods not produced anywhere are not required.

## 6. Era Lengths

**Eras are variable length, NOT fixed at 3 rounds.**

- [S4] "A new era starts if, at the beginning of the 'new era' phase, there are no companies of the previous era left to acquire, or if all the companies that are left are of one type."
- [S4] "Note that companies that can no longer start because there is no valid starting area left for them are discarded automatically, which may cause a new era to start earlier."
- [S1] Confirms the same condition-based era transition.

**Conclusion:** An era ends when all its company deeds are acquired, discarded, or only one type remains. This can happen in any number of rounds — eras are NOT fixed at 3 rounds each.

---

## 7. R&D Cost

**Advancing on an R&D track is FREE.**

- [S4] "In this phase, players may each research one out of 6 available upgrade types. The player researches an upgrade by placing his marker one step forward on the corresponding R&D track." No cost is mentioned.
- [S1] No cost mentioned for R&D advancement.
- [S2] No cost mentioned.

**Conclusion:** R&D advancement costs nothing. Players simply move their marker one step forward. There is no Rp 10 cost.

---

## 8. Phase Order

**The correct sequence of phases per year (round):**

1. **New Era** (if applicable)
2. **Bid for Turn Order**
3. **Mergers**
4. **Acquisitions**
5. **Research & Development**
6. **Operations**
7. **City Growth**

All four sources agree on this sequence: [S4][S1][S2][S3].

## 9. Free Expansion

**Free expansion is MANDATORY when all goods are sold.**

- [S4] "If a company has sold all its goods, that is, as many goods as it currently has on the board, it must expand for free."
- [S1] "If all goods were shipped, the company must expand for free a number of areas equal to the Expansions R&D."
- [S2] "If a company sells all the goods it produced it must expand for free."

**Conclusion:** Free expansion is mandatory (not optional) when a production company sells all its goods. The number of areas expanded equals the owner's Expansion R&D level. Expansion must follow placement rules (adjacent to existing production zone, no merging with same-type companies, empty land only).

---

## 10. Paid Expansion Cost

**Paid expansion costs the selling price per area of the good type.**

- [S4] "It can buy expansions... up to the number indicated on its R&D expansion track. The price per area is equal to that of the type of good the company is producing (e.g. 20 for each rice expansion, etc.)."
- [S1] "the player may choose to pay the selling price per area to expand up to the Expansions R&D."

**Cost table for paid expansion per area:**

| Good Type | Cost per Area |
|-----------|--------------|
| Rice      | 20 Rp        |
| Spice     | 25 Rp        |
| Rubber    | 30 Rp        |
| Siap Faji | 35 Rp        |
| Oil       | 40 Rp        |

**Conclusion:** Paid expansion is available only when NOT all goods were sold. Cost is the good's selling price per area. It is NOT a flat Rp 10. Maximum areas = Expansion R&D level.

## 11. Merger Bidder Requirements

**Yes, a bidder needs either a free company slot OR ownership of one of the merging companies.**

- [S4] "Every player with a free slot and both of the owners of the companies that are being merged may always join in the bidding."
- [S4] "every player who can own the resulting company may bid (i.e. the players with a free slot, and the owners of the merging companies)."
- [S1] "Own one of the two companies, or have Slots R&D greater than their company count."

**For ANNOUNCING a merger:**
- [S4] "he may only announce a merger if he is allowed to hold the resulting company. In practice, this means he has to own one of the merging companies, or have a free slot."
- Must have Mergers R&D >= resulting deed count. [S4]

**For BIDDING on a merger:**
- Must have a free slot OR own one of the merging companies. [S4]
- Must have sufficient cash. [S4]
- Merger R&D does NOT affect who may bid: "Merger R&D does NOT influence who may bid." [S4]

**Conclusion:** A free slot is required to bid on a merger UNLESS you already own one of the companies being merged. A merged company occupies only one slot, so an owner of a merging company effectively frees a slot by merging.

## 12. Scoring Tiebreaker

**Tiebreaker: the player who is first in the order of play wins.**

- [S4] "the player with the most money (including money in the bank) wins. In case of a tie, the player who is first on the order of play wins."
- [S2] "Ties go to the player first in turn order."

**Conclusion:** Tiebreaker is turn order position, NOT most cash (since cash is the primary scoring metric).

---

## 13. Scoring Pools / Money Tracking

**Three distinct money areas per player:**

1. **Cash (Supply)** — General spending money used for bids, paid expansion, etc.
2. **Bank (Turn Order Bid Stash)** — Money bid during Turn Order phase. Cannot be spent for the rest of the game but counts toward final score. [S4][S1][S2]
3. **Operating Revenue (Phase Earnings)** — Money earned during the current Operations phase. Kept separate during the final era because it is doubled at game end. [S4][S1]

**Final scoring formula:**
- Double the operating revenue from the final Operations phase.
- Add Cash + Bank + doubled operating revenue.
- Highest total wins. [S4][S1]

- [S4] "If the game continues to another round, move it to cash at the beginning of the next year."

## 14. Bankruptcy / IOU System

**The official rules [S4] do NOT describe an explicit IOU or bankruptcy/elimination system in detail.** However:

- [S4] References negative profit scenarios: "If you operate a company with negative profit (that is, it has to pay more for shipping than it earns), you must pay from this stack, not from your overall money. If there is no money in this phase's stack yet, write down the money you have to pay and take it out of the stack later in the phase."
- [S1] Mentions "take and pay off IOUs as needed" but without detailed rules.

**Conclusion:** Players are never eliminated. The rulebook implies a soft IOU system where debts within an Operations phase are tracked and settled later in the same phase. There is no formal bankruptcy or elimination mechanism described.

## 15. Shipping Cost Timing

**Shipping costs are deducted AFTER all goods for a company are shipped, from the operating revenue just earned.**

- [S4] The production company receives its income per good from the bank, then pays 5 Rp per ship used to shipping company owners. "The easiest way to keep track of this is to place 5 rupiah coins from the game on the different ships used and to give these coins to the owners afterwards, subtracting the sum from the amount earned by the production company."
- [S1] Sequence: (1) Bank pays production company, (2) total cargo cubes x 5 paid to bank, (3) cargo cubes distributed to ship owners at 5 each.

**Note on [S1] vs [S4]:** The Gist [S1] describes the production company paying the bank and then cubes being distributed to ship owners. The official rules [S4] describe paying the ship owners directly. The net economic effect is the same: production company revenue minus 5 per ship used = net income; ship owners receive 5 per ship used.

**Conclusion:** Revenue is received first, then shipping costs are paid. Both happen during the same company's operation.

## 16. Siap Faji Creation

**The new owner loses half (rounded up) of the total production areas.**

- [S4] "after the merger is completed, the new owner must give up half of the goods (land areas) owned by the old companies (rounded up). Empty these areas of any goods. You can only give up areas at the border of your company's estate—that is, you may not break contiguous zones of spice/rice by taking goods from the middle. Replace the rice/spice goods in the remaining areas with siap faji goods."
- [S1] "After creating a siap saji company from rice and spice, the new owner must remove half (rounded up) of the company's production areas on the board, and replace the remaining rice and spice with siap saji."

**Example from [S4]:** If merging 4 rice + 3 spice = 7 total areas, remove 4 (half of 7 rounded up), keep 3 as siap faji.

**Additional rules:**
- Can only remove border areas (cannot break contiguity). [S4]
- Cannot create siap faji in Era A — only in Era B or C. [S4]
- Once formed, siap faji can only merge with other siap faji, not with rice or spice. [S4]

## 17. Spelling: "Siap Faji" vs "Siap Saji"

**The official Splotter rulebook [S4] uses "siap faji" throughout.**

- [S4] Component list: "40 siap faji" markers. All rule text uses "siap faji."
- [S1] Uses "siap saji" (with an 's').
- [S2] Uses "Siap Faji."

**Conclusion:** The official spelling per the Splotter rulebook is **"siap faji"** (with an 'f'). Note: In Indonesian, "siap saji" (with an 's') means "ready to serve" and is the real-world term for fast food/microwave meals. "Siap faji" appears to be a deliberate Splotter variant or stylization. Both spellings are encountered in the community, but the rulebook uses "faji."

## 18. Hull Capacity

**Hull is a player-level R&D track, NOT a separate unlock at R&D level 3.**

- [S4] "Hull Player indicates the number of goods each ship belonging to a company of that player may transport for each production company during the operations phase."
- [S4] "Each player should place one ship of each of his shipping companies directly beneath his own marker on the Hull Company track, so that everyone can see at a glance what the capacity of each shipping company is."
- [S4] Players start with a marker on the first slot of each R&D track (including Hull).
- [S4] "A player may advance the hull size on another player's R&D track instead of advancing his own capabilities." This is unique to the Hull track — no other track can be advanced for another player.

**Conclusion:** Hull capacity is its own R&D track that each player advances independently. Starting capacity is 1 (first slot). Each advancement allows each ship to carry one additional good per production company. It is not "unlocked at R&D level 3" — it is always available and starts at level 1.

## 19. Company Types Table

| Type | Revenue per Good | Min Bid (Acquisition: free / Merger) | Paid Expansion Cost | Era Available |
|------|-----------------|--------------------------------------|--------------------:|:-------------:|
| **Shipping** | 5 Rp per ship used (paid by production co.) | Free to acquire; 10 Rp per ship for merger bid multiples | N/A (ship placement rules) | A, B, C |
| **Rice** | 20 Rp | Free to acquire; 20 Rp per area for merger bid multiples | 20 Rp/area | A |
| **Spice** | 25 Rp | Free to acquire; 25 Rp per area for merger bid multiples | 25 Rp/area | A |
| **Rubber** | 30 Rp | Free to acquire; 30 Rp per area for merger bid multiples | 30 Rp/area | B |
| **Siap Faji** (created) | 35 Rp | Created via merger at 25 Rp per area (total rice+spice areas) | 35 Rp/area | B, C |
| **Siap Faji** (merging two siap faji) | 35 Rp | 35 Rp per area for merger bid multiples | 35 Rp/area | B, C |
| **Oil** | 40 Rp | Free to acquire; 40 Rp per area for merger bid multiples | 40 Rp/area | C |

**Sources for revenue:** [S4] "20 for rice, 25 for spice, and so on (see the table on the player aid)." [S1] confirms: "20 rice | 25 spice | 30 rubber | 35 siap saji | 40 oil."

**Merger minimum bid multiples:** [S1] "10 shipping | 20 rice | 25 spice | 25 siap saji (create) | 30 rubber | 35 siap saji (merge) | 40 oil."

**Shipping company revenue:** Ships earn 5 Rp per good that passes through them, paid by the production company owner. [S4]

**Acquisition cost:** Starting a company is free — no money is paid. [S4] "Starting a company costs no money."

**Ship limits:** Each shipping company deed lists maximum ships per era (a/b/c). Merged shipping companies add their deed limits together. [S4]

## Key Discrepancies Between Sources

| Topic | Discrepancy | Resolution |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| Spelling | [S1] uses "siap saji"; [S4] uses "siap faji" | Official rulebook says "siap faji" |
| Shipping payment flow | [S1] describes paying bank then distributing cubes; [S4] describes paying ship owners directly | Net effect identical; [S4] is authoritative for the mechanism |
| Number of R&D tracks | [S2] says 6; [S4] describes 5 named tracks + company hull display | 5 player R&D tracks; hull display on company track is cosmetic |
| IOU rules | [S1] references IOUs; [S4] only mentions tracking debts within a phase | No formal IOU system in official rules; debts are phase-internal bookkeeping |
| Tiebreaker | [S1] silent; [S2] and [S4] agree on turn order | Turn order is the tiebreaker |
